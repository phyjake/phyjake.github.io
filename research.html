Atomic Structure of Xenon

AMO Physics Research Group · CU Denver · Ongoing

I'm modeling the atomic structure of xenon using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock method. Xenon has 54 electrons, which is enough that relativistic effects genuinely matter — the regular Schrödinger equation doesn't cut it, so we use the Dirac equation instead.

The Hartree-Fock part is a self-consistent field approach: each electron sees an average potential from all the others, and you iterate until the solution converges. It's a lot of computation and a lot of debugging, but I'm learning how big the gap is between textbook physics and physics you can actually run on a computer.

Why Xenon?

Heavy enough that relativistic effects matter, light enough that the computation doesn't take forever. A good testing ground.
