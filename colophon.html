On Using AI

This website was not coded by me; I used Claude by Anthropic to generate the code. I wanted a place where I could keep track of where I was as a student and person. A website felt like a pretty natural way to do this. It is important to me that I am upfront about this. I have a large amount of respect for people who can code and design a webpage like this. I am hoping one day to be able to comprehend every line of code involved in this website, but I am not there yet.

I would like to clarify that all of the writing on the site is my own; it won't be perfect or always very easy to follow, but it is my own words. I will likely be using a tool like Grammarly or Claude to help correct the grammar, but I will explicitly avoid having them alter my words. I think that would completely defeat the purpose of this website even if it makes me sound a little more clever or articulate.

I will be using Claude to help me improve as a writer. I will give it the things I typed and ask for criticism to improve my abilities. I will NEVER paste anything it has written or edited (beyond grammar) into the website; I will only use it to improve my ability to write unassisted.

I am someone that recognizes the potential of AI to assist in education and help develop new skills. My philosophy has always been, and will always be, that AI should never be anything more than a helpful guide. The line between helpful and harmful AI usage is thin, and I will continue to evaluate my usage to ensure it is helping me reach my goals. I want to be a competent person one day and I want to find the meaning and fun in getting good at something. I want AI to help me do this, not steal this from me.

Details

Built with: HTML, CSS, and a text editor. No frameworks, no JavaScript, no build tools.

Fonts: Newsreader, Space Mono, and Inter

Hosted on: GitHub Pages.

AI assistance: Claude (Anthropic) helped write the HTML and CSS. All written content is my own.
